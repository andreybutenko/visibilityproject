Visibility Project
Signs

Citygoers often want to help the homeless individuals they pass by, but face an information barrier. Do they want me to talk to them? What do they need? How can I help?

These signs are a call to action, encouraging citygoers to start conversations with the homeless individuals theyâ€™d otherwise pass by and ignore, developing relationships and providing support. They provide homeless individuals an opportunity to build connections and have conversations.

The signs are personalized, with a large blank space for an individual to write their unique needs and grab attention. The sign comes with a set of stickers indicating common needs, providing more context to those nearby. Identity is important, so there is an area for names as well.

Get Your Sign

We table at three shelters weekly across Seattle to help ensure we can help everyone who is interested.

  • Mondays, 1pm-2pm. Pioneer Square Union Gospel Mission: 318 2nd Avenue Extension S.
  • Wednesdays, 1pm-2pm. U-District Urban Rest Stop: 1415 NE 43rd Street.
  • Fridays, 1pm-2pm. Belltown Millionair Club: 2515 Western Ave.
