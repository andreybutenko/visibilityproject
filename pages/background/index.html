Homelessness:
Background and Myths

What are some causes of homelessness?

Lack of Affordable Housing

In King County, it is difficult for low-income individuals and families to find housing. For a two-bedroom unit in King County, a worker must earn over $17 per hour to afford, making it nearly unattainable for poor families.

Limited Access to Mental Health Support

Mental illness is an affliction which can affect anyone in a severe way, making individuals unable to care of necessary daily responsibilities and limit their ability to form stable relationships. Mental health services combat both mental illness and homelessness, making it an important policy issue.

Lack of support

A single crisis can bring an individual to homelessness. An injury, a sick child, or a car breakdown can lead to losing a job then losing a home. Families, churches, neighborhoods, and other community resources can act as a support network

What are common misconceptions about homelessness?

A job is all it takes to get out of homelessness

Many homeless individuals do work and make some money, it just may not be enough to afford housing. Especially in places with high costs of living, there are other, sometimes more urgent needs: food, clothing, etc.

Fighting homelessness is expensive

Housing costs $10,000 per person per year. The costs associated with homelessness are far greater: law enforcement, hospitals, and community services average $31,000 per year.

What are the needs of homeless individuals?

Housing

Housing is important: a sense of physical security is important in enabling an individual to flourish. However, most of Seattle's housing facilities are short-term and just 8% of individuals who use these emergency housing services find permanent housing afterwards.

Healthcare

Some homeless individuals with disabilities can receive healthcare through Medicare or Medicaid, but the application process is complex and takes months to work through. This worsens their existing conditions, requiring emergency room care when a medical crisis finally occurs.

Human Connection

It is hard to know who to trust and difficult to make connections. Often, homeless individuals go ignored by others. Still, these individuals have much to say to those who are willing to listen.
