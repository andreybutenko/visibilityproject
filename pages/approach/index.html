Approaching,
Having a Conversation

Starting a conversation with a homeless individual is easy: just walk up and start talking! Still, some people get nervous doing this. Here is a list of frequently asked questions that may help you.

How do I know who's approachable?

Look for individuals who are facing the sidewalk. A good sign is if they're holding a sign! They may also be just sitting on a bench or leaning against a wall. Never approach anyone who's sleeping or facing away from you.

How do I start a conversation?

Start a conversation the same way you would any other! Say "hello," introduce yourself, and ask the other person how they're doing.

What should I talk about?

Anything! In terms of questions you might like to explore, you can ask what they're working towards, how they became homeless, or what their needs are right now. If they mention an interest they have, you can ask more about it.

What if I mess up? What questions should I avoid?

Showing that you care and that you're willing to listen is all you need to do. Don't be concerned with "correctness;" all you need is to have a conversation. You might be the only person the individual talks to that day, so it will be important no matter what.
